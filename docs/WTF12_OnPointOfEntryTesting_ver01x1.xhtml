Point of Entry Testing
Viktor Shkolnikov
With the current pandemic, there is a sentiment that the pandemic could have been prevented by testing individuals entering the country and quarantining those testing positive. Here I explore how feasible is this with current tests and what test specifications are necessary to make this feasible.
Let's start with how would point of entry testing and quarantining look like at the beginning of an epidemic. Only when about 1 in 100 people have the disease, will you be roughly quarantining one healthy person per one person with the disease. This is probably acceptable. For the US healthcare system, there is a total of about 1 million hospital beds; order 10% of them are meant for intensive care. There is order 300 million people in US. Therefore the US is roughly equipped to handle 0.3% of its population in inpatient care and 0.03% in intensive care. Therefore, if 1 in 100 of the people that have the disease need intensive care, when 1 in 100 have a disease, there is an additional 0.01% of the population that requires intensive care, or 30% of the intensive care capacity.
